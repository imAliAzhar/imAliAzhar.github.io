ABOUT ME

I'm a fourth year Software Engineering undergraduate student from National University of Sciences and Technology. My main area of interests are Machine Learning and Distributed Computing.

I have worked as a research intern at NUST-TUKL Lab where I have worked on propriety projects focused primarily on Deep Learning and Computer Vision problems. My most recent project at TUKL included rewriting Caffe code in TensorRT which is high-performance deep learning inference optimizer for NVIDIA Jetson inference modules. I have used NVIDIA DIGITS and Keras to train models on CPU and GPUs in the past.

Python combined with Numpy and Pandas is my primary framework for rapid prototyping. I implemented machine learning algorithms (KNN, Linear Classifier, Neural Networks, CNN) in Numpy, and tested the implementation on CIFAR-10 dataset as part of Stanford’s CS231n course. My side projects range from online games to web based applications.

I would describe myself as highly determined and ambitious. I like to be in the driving seat of all my projects. I appreciate feedback from team members and regularly ask them for it. I love taking up challenging projects that push me to my limits.

I’m an avid reader. You can find me on Quora here. I also love tinkering with different Linux distros.
